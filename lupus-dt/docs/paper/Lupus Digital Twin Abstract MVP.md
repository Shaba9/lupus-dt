# Abstract

## An Explainable Gene-Expression-Based Digital Twin for Identing Immune Tolerance Restoration Targets in Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE)

Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE) is a complex autoimmune disease characterized by significant biological heterogeneity, making diagnosis, treatment selection, and long-term disease management challenging. Although recent advances in artificial intelligence, transcriptomics, and precision medicine have enabled improved disease stratification and biomarker discovery, most existing approaches focus on disease prediction, flare forecasting, or treatment response estimation rather than identifying the underlying mechanisms responsible for immune dysregulation.

This project proposes a minimum viable product (MVP) for an explainable, gene-expression-based digital twin designed to identify potential immune tolerance restoration targets in SLE. The proposed framework creates a virtual representation of a patient's immune state using publicly available transcriptomic datasets from healthy controls and lupus patients. By establishing a healthy immune reference model and comparing it against patient-specific gene-expression profiles, the system quantifies disease-state deviations at the pathway level.

A virtual perturbation engine is then used to simulate the restoration or suppression of abnormal biological pathways and estimate their impact on movement toward a healthy immune state. The platform ranks candidate pathways according to their predicted contribution to disease and their potential value as therapeutic intervention targets. Rather than acting as a diagnostic or treatment system, the platform functions as a research and discovery tool that generates biologically interpretable hypotheses for future laboratory and clinical investigation.

The primary contribution of this work is a software-only digital twin framework that combines transcriptomic analysis, pathway-level modeling, virtual experimentation, and explainable target prioritization within a single platform. The proposed MVP serves as a foundation for future immune-system digital twins capable of integrating multi-omics, clinical, and immunological data. Ultimately, this research aims to support the discovery of personalized immune tolerance restoration strategies and contribute to future precision medicine approaches for autoimmune diseases.
